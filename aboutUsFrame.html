ABOUT US

Introduction

Mzamomtsha Primary School is a public primary school located at 49 Valangentambo Street. It is one of the public primary schools in urban Driftsands suburb, City of Cape Town in the Western Cape province of the Republic of South Africa. Mzamomtsha Primary School is located between Khayelistha Township and Umfuleni Township. It is a section 21 school with ABE functions and classified under quintile 3 as a no fee institution. The school EMIS number is 106461792 and postal address at PO Box 40034, Elonwabeni, 7791. The school was initially created for primary school leaners from grade 1 to grade 7, but due to the rapid population growth the school also enrolling high grades up to grade 12.

The school was initially created for primary school students from grade 1 to grade 7, but due to the rapid population growth (births and migration) the school is also enrolling higher grades. Students are allowed up to grade 12 - which is the matric year.

Vision

Our vision at Mzamomtsha Primary School is to empower students to acquire, demonstrate, articulate and value knowledge and skills that will support them, as life-long learners, to participate in and contribute to the global world and practice the core values of the school: respect, tolerance & inclusion, and excellence.

Mission

Our mission is to develop students with active and creative minds, a sense of understanding and compassion for others, and the courage to act on their beliefs. We stress the total development of each child: spiritual, moral, intellectual, social, emotional, and physical.

Frequently asked questions about our school:

Does This School Have A Particular Educational Philosophy?

This school sees its uniqueness in allowing teachers the ability to teach their students any way they see is appropriate to achieve their full potential in academics.

How Do Administrators Evaluate Teachers?

Teachers are evalulated based on the performance of the classes they teach, if a class is seen as lacking in progress the teacher will be held responsible and be dealt with if needed.

How Much Does Lunch Cost?

Lunch is free for children at this school, but we also have a tuck shop which provides children with additional sweets and lunches such as burgers and sandwiches at addition costs.

Do The Kids Get Recess Every Day?

Yes, there is a total of 2 recess periods eveyday, a 25 minute recess is given after the third period, and a 15 minute recess is given after the sixth period of the day.

How Many Kids Are In A Class?

Currently we have about 700 pupils being given lessons be only 20 teachers in total. That means that roughly 35 children are in each class, but we aim to reduce that number in the future by building more classrooms and hiring more teachers to lessons the workload of the current teachers.

Is There A Dress Code?

No, our school is a symbol of the freedom of creativity found in this country, and how our students dress is a reflection of that. We only ask that no inappropriate attire be worn.

Biography

Mr A.Raboshada (44)

Mr Raboshada was born on the 25/05/1975 in Greyton. He was the youngest of 4 children and attended school in the local area. He was mischievous growing up and almost failed matric were it not for a teacher who patiently sat with him thrice a week and helped him revise. After successfully achieving his matric he took a gap year, uncertain as to what he wanted to do his mind went back to the teacher who helped him and his choice was made.

He acquired his bachelors in education at UCT and taught at an all-boys high school for 6 years before acquiring a masters in education in leadership.

Raboshada has always had good relationships with students. This could be down to his love of sport and athletics and the vibrant competitive nature he has, but more often than not it’s his calm and caring approach towards students that earns him such an exceptional reputation. Rabashoda has been headmaster here since April 2018 and is continuing in his attempts to better education as a whole.
